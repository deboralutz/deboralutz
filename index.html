Structural Political Theory & Hybrid Systems Governance

I develop a long-term research program on the minimal structural grammar of governability under conditions of distributed human and artificial agency.

This work articulates Field, Coherence, and Limit as irreducible structural conditions required for political order to remain intelligible, accountable, and sustainable under technological transformation.

The project integrates conceptual analysis, institutional design, and formal governance architectures across hybrid socio-technical systems.

Research

My current research advances a structural grammar of governability applicable to hybrid socio-technical systems. It examines how relational configurations, dynamic compatibility, and differentiated authority interact to sustain political order under technological transformation.

The Triadic Theory of Governability articulates the minimal structural architecture required for governance frameworks themselves to remain viable.
