Débora Mariane da Silva Lutz

Structural Political Theory & Hybrid Systems Governance

Independent researcher in structural political theory and hybrid systems governance.

My work examines the minimal structural conditions under which governability can persist in contexts of distributed human and artificial agency.

My research develops a triadic structural model of governability — Field, Coherence, and Limit — as irreducible conditions for relational intelligibility, temporal sustainability, and differentiated authority.

The focus of my work is the preservation of accountable power under conditions of algorithmic mediation, institutional complexity, and hybrid human–AI systems.

Research

My current research advances a structural grammar of governability applicable to hybrid socio-technical systems. It examines how relational configurations, dynamic compatibility, and differentiated authority interact to sustain political order under technological transformation.

The Triadic Theory of Governability articulates the minimal structural architecture required for governance frameworks themselves to remain viable.
