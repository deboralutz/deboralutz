Research

Structural Political Theory

My research develops a structural account of governability under conditions of distributed and hybrid human–AI agency. It examines the minimal conditions under which political order remains intelligible, accountable, and sustainable over time.

The central contribution is the Triadic Theory of Governability (TTG), which articulates three irreducible structural dimensions:

  • Field — relational intelligibility of agency
  • Coherence — temporal sustainability of systemic dynamics
  • Limit — differentiated authority and accountability

These dimensions form a minimal structural grammar required for governance frameworks to remain viable under technological transformation.

Research Program

The research program advances a structural account of governability applicable across institutional, technological, and legal domains.

Current axes of development include:

  • Triadic structural theory of governability
  • Institutional translation of structural conditions
  • Hybrid human–AI authority differentiation
  • Living authorship and evolving governance architectures

The long-term objective is the consolidation of a coherent structural framework for accountable power under distributed agency.
