The Triadic Architecture of Governability

Abstract

This paper advances a structural theory of governability under conditions of distributed and hybrid human–AI agency. It argues that governability is not secured by performance, optimization, or regulatory compliance, but depends upon three irreducible structural conditions: Field, Coherence, and Limit.

Field designates the relational configuration within which agency becomes politically intelligible. Coherence concerns the temporal sustainability of systemic dynamics across heterogeneous layers. Limit differentiates authority and preserves the conditions of accountability.

Together, these elements form a minimal structural grammar of political order under technological transformation. The paper demonstrates the mutual constitution, non-substitutability, and categorial necessity of the triadic model, and examines its implications for institutional design in hybrid systems.
